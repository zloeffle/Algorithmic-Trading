Relative Strength Index

Overview

Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum indicator that measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought and oversold conditions in the price of an asset. RSI scores range from 0-100, an asset is considered overbought (Sell signal) when RSI is greater than 70 and oversold (Buy signal) when RSI is less than 30.

Bollinger Bands

Overview

The Bollinger Bands indicator is composed of three lines, a middle band, upper band, and lower band. The middle band represents the simple moving average of an asset's price over a specified period, typically 20 days. The upper and lower bands are typically set to be +/- 2 standard deviations away from the middle band. The idea is that the closer prices move towards the upper band, the more oversold the market is and the closer they move towards the lower band, the more oversold the market is. Standard deviation is a way to measure market volatility. As the bands widen, this represents this represents a more volatile market and when the bands contract, this represents a less volatile market.
